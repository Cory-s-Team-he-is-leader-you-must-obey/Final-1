Discrimination against disabled people in the workplace and in school

Oftentimes workers with disabilities are fired or mistreated due to their conditions. These workers are being set up for failure with their workplace or job requirements not being catered to their disability, which results in the stereotype that they cannot perform in the workplace. One of the most common ways people with disabilities are discriminated against, whether intentionally or not, is failing to properly accommodate employees with disabilities. However, hiring people with disabilities is a win-win-win situation for the individual, the company, and the economy. People with disabilities achieve acceptance and independence while also being financially secure and their contribution helps the economy. Now switching over to the educational system, there is not enough educational aid and attention put on these children that come from underprivileged homes. Disabled students without someone to help them have a much harder road to education than non-disabled students. An experiment held in schools in Zambia showed that children with disabilities in school are more likely to experience violence within school.

About

There is a overall moral obligation that society holds to stop discrimination of minority groups and stop the mistreatment of people with disabilities. Especially when they can contribute to society in so many positive ways. Sadly, many times society doesn’t give them a chance to. In 2016, “A woman with dyslexia won a disability discrimination case against her employer Starbucks after she was accused of falsifying documents,” according to the BBC. In this instance, this woman was given an unrealistic task of keeping records of the company, when her condition causes her to struggle with reading, writing, and telling time. Then in turn was blamed and fired without a second thought. While Starbucks was called out for this discrimination, many cases just like this go unnoticed, with people with disabilities being fired for things they have no control over. Businesses as well as society as a whole are unfairly discriminating against people with disabilities. If society in turn was more accepting and set up disabled people for success instead of treating them like burdens, it would be able to see just how many ways disabled people can be positive influences on society..

Education for disabled children

This image depicts many statistics about children with disabilities. It lists information regarding the impact the schooling system has on these children. They are 10 times less likely to attend school compared to a child without a disability. Many children with disabilities are often bullied and treated differently in school. They should not be persecuted for something they cannot control. Children with disabilities are one of the most marginalized and excluded groups of children, and are often ‘invisible’ due to high levels of stigma, poverty and vulnerability. Disability has a greater impact on access to education than gender, household economic status, or rural/urban divide. There needs to be proper care and staff for children with disabilities. There needs to be a change in the schooling systems. Children with disabilities deserve better and need an equal chance of getting the proper education needed to succeed in this life.

Disability and inclusion in the workplace

Like the video says, disability does not reflect or define capability.This video does a great job of explaining why disabled people should not be discriminated against in the workplace. It uses an example very similar to the “don’t judge a fish by its ability to climb a tree” quote from Albert Einstein, and it makes perfect sense here. Some disabled people have been through a lot more difficulty in their lives and are even more capable of doing some things than fully able bodied people. Finally the video closes with a really important statement: don't think about what someone can’t do for you, think creatively and find something that they can do or might be even more adept at doing. This video does a spectacular job in helping create a more inclusive working envrionment.

Teachers’ Responses to School Violence Against Students With Disabilities

Click here to open this PDF in a new window[BS13]


Around the world, school violence is an epidemic and even greater amongst students with disabilities. An experiment held in schools in Zambia showed that children with disabilities in school are more likely to experience violence within school. Those students felt safer and more protected with special education teachers rather than general education teachers. However the special education staff were pressured to take less action when in situations by other teachers. This discrimination and lack of help has led to more violence and bullying towards disabled students, with little staff assisting them and making them feel safe in school environments.
